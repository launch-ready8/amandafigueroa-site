The challenge

Demo costs above $1,500, and some campaigns weren't converting at all.

This client had a B2B healthcare SaaS product — the kind of tool that gets used across providers, payers, pharmacies, and everyone adjacent. On paper, the demand was there. But their previous agency had paid search running, and it was costing more than $1,500 just to land a single demo request. A few campaigns weren't converting at all.

By the time they came to me, the conversation inside the company had quietly shifted from “how do we fix this?” to “should we just turn these off?” — on the assumption that the demand simply wasn't there for those segments. Before anyone pulled the plug, I wanted to find out whether that was actually true, or whether something in the setup was quietly smothering it.

The approach

Treat search intent as the actual lever.

My hunch was that the targeting was off, not that the demand was missing. So I went through the whole account, end to end.

I rewrote the ads so they matched what people were actually typing into Google. I tightened the keywords around exact-match, high-intent terms instead of casting a wide net and paying for noise. And I rewrote the landing pages so the whole journey — search result to page to call-to-action — finally felt like one coherent thing, instead of handing high-intent visitors a generic page and hoping.

I also reworked the bidding so it reflected what each kind of conversion was really worth. And for the campaigns Google had been feeding bad data for months, I relaunched them from scratch — letting the algorithm start from a clean slate instead of optimizing toward the wrong outcome.

The turn

The campaigns they were ready to kill? Those turned out to be the most rewarding — once we stopped fighting their bad history.
