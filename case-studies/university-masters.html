Case Study · Higher Education

Growing graduate applications 20% in a declining sector.

A master's program built a domestic acquisition engine for career-pivoting professionals.

The challenge

Domestic growth in a declining sector.

Graduate applications were down sector-wide. The program had a strong international pipeline and an active paid media program, but leadership couldn't get a clear read on what was actually driving applications. They wanted cleaner ROI on the existing spend, plus volume growth from a new audience: US-based, career-pivoting professionals the program was designed for.

The approach

Lead with the career outcome.

We rebuilt the paid acquisition program across LinkedIn, Meta, and Google. The bigger shift was in the creative: instead of leading with rankings and faculty bios, the ads led with what graduates did with the degree and the careers they moved into.

We built audiences off professional intent signals (job title shifts, industry transitions, certification interest) rather than broad demographic targeting. In the first 60 days we tested aggressive variants, cut the bottom half by week 8, and doubled down on what worked.

Each month the program team saw exactly what was performing, what was dragging, and what we'd cut next.

The results

A five-year application high in a year the sector declined.
